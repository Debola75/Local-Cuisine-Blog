Tom Yum

About Tom Yum

Tom Yum is one of Thailand's most iconic dishes, known for its hot and sour flavor profile. This aromatic soup has been a staple of Thai cuisine for centuries and is celebrated worldwide for its perfect balance of spicy, sour, and savory elements.

Ingredients

Aromatics

  • 4 stalks lemongrass, bruised
  • 4 slices galangal
  • 3-4 kaffir lime leaves

Protein & Broth

  • 1 lb shrimp or chicken
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth

Seasonings

  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1-2 red chilies, sliced
  • 1 tbsp Thai chili paste

Vegetables

  • 8 oz mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 medium tomato, quartered
  • 2 tbsp cilantro

Instructions

  1. Bring broth to a boil in a large pot
  2. Add lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves
  3. Simmer for 5 minutes to infuse flavors
  4. Add Thai chili paste and stir well
  5. Add shrimp or chicken and cook for 3-5 minutes
  6. Add mushrooms and tomato, simmer for 2 minutes
  7. Season with fish sauce and lime juice
  8. Garnish with cilantro and chilies
  9. Serve hot in bowls

  • Add lime juice just before serving for maximum flavor
  • Fresh ingredients make a huge difference in taste
  • Remove aromatics before serving if you prefer a cleaner broth

  • Vegetarian: Replace protein with tofu and use vegetable broth
  • Creamier: Add coconut milk for a richer texture
  • Extra Spicy: Add more Thai chili paste or fresh chilies
